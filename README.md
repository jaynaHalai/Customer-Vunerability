# Customer-Vunerability Prediction

## Overview

According to the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), a vulnerable customer is someone who, due to their personal circumstances, is especially susceptible to harm. This is especially true if a company does not treat them with the proper level of care.

The FCA views vulnerability as a spectrum of risk rather than a permanent label. Anyone can experience vulnerability at any time. It can be permanent, temporary, or come and go depending on what is happening in a person's life

The 4 Key Drivers of Vulnerability:

The FCA groups the causes of vulnerability into four main categories:

1. Health: Severe or long-term illnesses, physical disabilities, mental health conditions, or cognitive changes that make day-to-day tasks harder.
2. Life Events: Major life changes such as losing a loved one, a relationship breakdown, losing a job, or taking on new caring responsibilities.
3. Resilience: A low ability to withstand financial or emotional shocks, which could be due to low savings, high debt, or an unstable income.
4. Capability: A low knowledge of financial matters, low confidence with numbers, or struggling with digital tools, literacy, and language barriers.

Identifying customer vunerability has become a challange and therefore this project develops a machine learning model to predict customer vunerability.
